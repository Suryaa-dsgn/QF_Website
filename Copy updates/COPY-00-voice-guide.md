# COPY-00 — Voice & Tone Guide
## The master reference for all copy on Quickflows.ai

> **Give this file to Claude Code at the start of every copy-related session.**
> Every string of text on this site — headlines, labels, descriptions, button text,
> tooltips, placeholder text — must follow these rules.

---

## Who Is Reading This

**Primary:** C-suite — CIOs, CCOs, CFOs, VPs of Operations.
People who evaluate software for a living. They've seen hundreds of AI company pitches.
They have pattern recognition for generic marketing language.
They do not read websites — they scan them for signals of competence and credibility.

**Secondary:** Compliance teams and investors.
They visit to validate the company, not to discover the product.
They want to see domain expertise, honest claims, and a real team.

**What these people respond to:**
Specificity. Precision. Someone who clearly understands their operational reality.
They trust companies that sound like they know what a DEA registration expiry
costs a healthcare system — not companies that talk about "operational efficiency."

**What immediately loses them:**
Generic AI buzzwords. Corporate mission statements. Unverifiable statistics.
Anything that sounds like it was written by a language model and not edited by a human.

## The Voice in One Sentence

**Dry confidence.** We know this works. We don't need to oversell it.

---

## Voice Characteristics

### 1. Specific over general — always
❌ "Streamline your operational workflows"
✅ "Shift gaps filled before your team wakes up"

❌ "Improve efficiency across your financial operations"
✅ "Your AR team shouldn't be your reconciliation team"

❌ "Reduce operational costs significantly"
✅ "A shift filled by an agency costs $400 more than one filled internally"

### 2. Active product as subject — not the company
❌ "Quickflows helps your team reduce manual work"
✅ "Quickflows fills the shift. Matches the invoice. Flags the credential."

The product does things. It doesn't "help" people do things.
"Help" implies it's a tool. It's an agent. Show it acting.

### 3. Short sentences. Then longer ones when needed. Then short again.
This creates rhythm. One thought per sentence in most cases.
When something needs nuance, use a longer sentence — then cut back.
Never more than three connected clauses in a row.

### 4. Peer language — words a CIO uses in their own status updates
❌ "Our innovative AI solution leverages cutting-edge technology"
✅ "Agents trained on your actual shift history and compliance rules"

❌ "Seamless integration with your existing tech stack"
✅ "Works with Epic, Workday, QuickBooks, and whatever else you're already running"

### 5. Restraint — don't explain everything
If a sentence is doing too much explaining, it's trying to compensate
for a weak claim. Strong claims are short. They trust the reader.

❌ "Our AI agents automatically detect and resolve operational gaps in real time,
    enabling your team to focus on strategic priorities instead of manual tasks."
✅ "Gaps get filled. Your team focuses on the exceptions."

### 6. The negative construction — what your team shouldn't have to do
This is one of the strongest patterns in the site's copy.
Naming what someone shouldn't be doing implies competence about their world.

✅ "Your coordinators shouldn't spend their mornings on the phone."
✅ "Your AR team shouldn't be your reconciliation team."
✅ "A credential expiry shouldn't be something your team catches manually."

## Words Never to Use

The following words are banned. Completely. In any context.

```
streamline          optimize / optimise    leverage
revolutionize       transform             cutting-edge
state-of-the-art   innovative            seamless
robust              scalable              synergy
empower             holistic              strategic
world-class         best-in-class         game-changing
disruptive          next-generation       AI-powered (as a standalone claim)
```

Also banned:
- Exclamation marks. Anywhere. On any page.
- "Our mission is to..."
- "Built for X, by X"
- "In today's [anything]"
- "We're passionate about..."
- "At Quickflows, we believe..."
- Any sentence that starts with "We" and ends with a value claim

## Punctuation Rules

**Em dash (—):** Use deliberately. Not more than once per paragraph.
Signals a pivot or a reveal. "Shift gaps filled. Invoices matched. Credentials renewed —
before anyone has to ask."

**Ellipsis (...):** Avoid. Sounds hesitant.

**Semicolons:** Rarely. When two related facts need to stay together.

**Oxford comma:** Always.

**Question marks:** Use sparingly. Only when genuinely asking the reader something.
"Don't see your industry? We're likely building it." — this one works.

## The Narrative Arc of the Homepage

Every section has a specific job in the story.
Copy in each section must serve that job — nothing more.

| Section | Story Job | Wrong version | Right version |
|---------|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| Hero | The solution exists. | "We automate operations." | "They're already handled." |
| The Problem | You recognise your world here. | "Companies struggle with manual work." | "Coordinator on hold at 6:03am." |
| Vertical Cards | This is which door is yours. | "Learn about our products." | "Your AR team shouldn't be your reconciliation team." |
| Dark Scope | The scale is real. | "We've built a lot." | Three numbers. No sentences needed. |
| Industries | Your sector is covered. | "We serve many industries." | "Healthcare, finance, and compliance don't get second chances." |
| Social Proof | Other operators trust this. | "Customers love Quickflows." | Numbers framed as targets + human quotes. |
| How It Works | The path to live is short. | "Our process is easy." | "One call. Agents running in weeks." |
| CTA | One clear next step. | "Get started today!" | "Book a 20-minute call." |

## A Note on Copy That Isn't Here Yet

**Testimonials:** The current quotes are illustrative placeholders. When real client quotes
are available, use them exactly as spoken — even if the grammar is imperfect.
Real imperfect quotes outperform polished fabricated ones every time.

**Client logos:** When real logos are added to the trust bar, remove the company names
entirely. Logos don't need labels.

**Founder bios:** When real photos and bios are added to /about, write them in first or
third person, and lead with one specific sentence about their background — not a title.
"Shudhanshu spent five years in healthcare operations before building Quickflows."
Not: "Shudhanshu is an experienced entrepreneur passionate about AI."
